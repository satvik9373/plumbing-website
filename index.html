Why is my sink draining slowly?

A slow-draining sink is often caused by a buildup of debris, grease, hair, or soap scum in the pipes. This can restrict the flow of water. To address this issue, you can try using a plunger or a drain snake to remove the blockage. If the problem persists, it's best to call a professional plumber to assess and fix the issue.

What should I do if I have a water leak in my home?

To prevent pipes from freezing during winter, you can take several precautions:

Insulate exposed pipes with foam insulation sleeves.
Allow a small trickle of water to flow through faucets during very cold weather.
Keep your home adequately heated, even if you're away.
Seal any gaps or cracks in walls, floors, and foundation to prevent cold air from entering.

Can I install new outlets in my home myself?

While some basic electrical tasks can be tackled by homeowners, like changing a light switch, installing new outlets is more complex. It involves working with wiring and connections, which can be dangerous if not done correctly. It's recommended to hire a qualified electrician to ensure the installation is up to code and safe.
